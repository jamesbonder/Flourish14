About Rosanna's Work

cu•ri•o (ˈkyʊər iˌoʊ) n., pl. -ri•os.
Something unusual - perhaps worthy of collecting

Beachcombing is instinctive and reminiscent of childhood and home. The act of searching the shore for shells and the impulse to pick up these objects was natural, but only the ones I felt a fascination or curiosity about. As I accumulated my collection of shells, I found myself beginning to organise each one, forming individual patterns and orderly lines leading to the creation of arranged displays. Each shell would be laid carefully onto the cotton bed sheet where it felt right. Completing the process, the display is photographed - this is now its only existence. This fixed state makes them permanent and reflects my thoughts and feelings at their time of creation. Whilst some may be similar, no display is ever the same, making their impermanence imperative.

Searching, gathering, cleaning, categorising, arranging, positioning.

The process of collecting becomes one of escape; of quiet and calm – a time I have allocated for myself. By creating serenity, this precious activity becomes a way of dealing with my skin condition. The irritation is temporarily suppressed. Through arranging these natural objects, my body comes into contact with the shell of another life; another body. Surfaces touch, scratch, rub and smooth together. The softness of the bed sheet comforts the harsh, hard bodies of the shells. Creases form in the sheet over time as the process carries on. The creases hold its age, showing comfort, but comfort is not perfect.
