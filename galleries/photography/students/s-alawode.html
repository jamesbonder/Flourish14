About Stephen's Work

This project examines the definition of gender and how male or female sexed individuals can be gendered differently. The work looks at how we struggle with the idea of gender being a social construct because we find it quite difficult to dissociate from childhood gender values that are continuously reinforced by the society. This project reveals the actuality that gender is in fact self-defined and it is nothing more than an elaborate performance done a daily bases. Gender is subconsciously constructed and a closer observation of the day-to-day revels gender to be a theatrical performance, this project features a series of images about the performance and play acting of gender

Gender also has a great impact on social identities and our social identities play a very vital role in our self-defined gender and our fashion/ clothing choices. This is because, fashion/clothes are involved in the construction of our self-image, gender identity development, self-expression and self-presentation. This project uses fashion to construct new identities for individuals who already have self-defined genders, this was done because fashion is morphing into something more complex, a means of social communication that informs other about how we view ourselves and how we would like to be perceived by others.
