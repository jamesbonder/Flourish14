About Miriam's Work

This work explores the rural community and landscapes of the Snowdonia National Park, whilst taking as a starting point my own identity through an inherited life style of the farms I grew up around.

There is a unique relationship that resides between places, people, animals and the land that has shaped who I am and continues to exert a strong influence on my life today. A sense of nostalgia and romanticism has been explored through a series of still lives that hark back to my childhood and the fond memories of living on a farm and being looked after by my grandparents.

The landscapes of my childhood remains as both a link to my past and a continual reminder of where I learnt from my parents and grandparents the importance of an inherited lifestyle.

The portraits look at the extended family that work the farms close to where I grew up. These people, along with the landscapes and still lives, are central to a strong community spirit and sense of place that has influenced my upbringing in this corner of the old county of Merioneth and continues to shape me as a person today.
