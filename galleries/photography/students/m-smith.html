Meg Smith

BA (Hons) Photography for Fashion and Advertising


About Meg's Work

Martin Scorsese once said, “Cinema is a matter of what’s in the frame and what’s out.” In this work I wanted to capture the essence of iconic movies in one photograph. I wanted the photographs to capture elements of the films that would create conversations and some photographs contain elements that some viewers may have missed in the film.

Each photograph represents one feature length film and some are focusing on a particular iconic prop, others on iconic scenes. I chose to work with a wide range of films from numerous genres, but I wanted to ensure I created a coherent series of photographs.

To ensure that all the images look like part of the same collection, post-production and lighting was key. I also wanted the viewer to focus on the prop or scene, so I chose to use as little of a human presence in the photographs as possible.

I chose to make the entire series portrait as this allows me to create prints that can resemble classic film posters.
