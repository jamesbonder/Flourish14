About Kirstie's Work

Hi, my name is Kirstie and I am currently finishing my final year of Photographic Art at The University of South Wales, formerly known as The University of Wales, Newport. I was born in South Africa but moved to the UK at the age of 10. Along with photography, travelling is part of who I am and what I love doing. At the age of 7 I was given my first camera. Although I do not remember my first photographs, I do know that I have the same desire to use my camera as I did at the age of 7.

My current project, ‘Our Desire for Beauty’, explores the relationship between descriptive views and the pictorial views of a town. This project looks into the authenticity of a walking tour of Oxford and just how the tourists views are shaped by what the guide is pointing the tourist towards, rather than what is right beside or behind them. The project takes a step back and looks around.

The final pieces for this project use comments made by the guide, on the walking tour of Oxford, about a particular building or area, which are then paired with a photograph taken at that very spot. The photograph does not focus on the attraction being shown, instead what a tourist would prefer not to see during a visit. The viewer is then left with the reality of what is right behind or alongside these destinations.
