About Annabelle's Work

I am a passionate young photographer who has the sophisticated eye of a photojournalist, and the ability to see and produce images that are truly unique. My passion for photography and the evolution of modern society inspire and drive me to acknowledge notable moments in life. I believe these moments deserve to be preserved forever through an image that can be loved by generations. I want to pursue a career in photography because I know my images will influence someone, by giving them the ability to perceive something they don't normally see.

IMy final year project has been a photographic documentation on the gradual decline of Working Men's Clubs. These private social clubs are sadly disappearing in today's society, they have been around since the 19th century and have played a substantial part in welsh traditions. They were once thriving hubs for working class communities, places for industrial workers to relax, socialise, have a drink, and be entertained. There is a real sense of belonging within these clubs, and if they do eventually die out another fabric of Welsh valley life will go with them.

This work has been compiled from three different Working Men’s Clubs, Abertysswg Working Men’s Club, Trallwn Workmen’s Club and Canton Liberal Workmen’s Club. Whilst acknowledging the decline of such institutions, the work also celebrates the community spirit to be found within them. It is this community spirit that is at threat as we move into the twenty-first century.
