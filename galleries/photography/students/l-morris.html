Lottie Morris

BA (Hons) Photographic Art


About Lottie's Work

Lottie Morris' work mainly revolves around ideas of adding to something already existing. By inserting objects, creating an installation and documenting the outcome the work is playful, elaborate and defined.

Her final graduate work entitled Plenitude explores the plethora of countless digital archives. Infinite intangible photographs are floating around the digisphere; rarely revisited. The advancement of technology in the twenty-first century means we can now unlimitedly create, consume and contribute photographs. These individual photos capture specific moments that might be important but the reality is a multitude of digital files, often stored away from sight.

Exploring these notions Lottie Morris starts to deal with her own personal photographic archive, taken on her iPhone, possessing over 10,000 files, she takes those from the past year, deals with them physically and invents a new visual interpretation. Overwhelmed by the sheer volume of images she illustrates the obsession with documenting life through photographs, creating an over-saturated archive.

Transforming the nonmaterial digital space, making the photographs physical objects and throwing them into the world becomes an extensive pursuit. The trawling through, the uploading, the delivering, the physical weight and the ritual of reframing the images as an unfamiliar documentation, represents an overwhelming task. The resulting installation looks almost hyper-real when photographed and presents a compulsion for relentlessly documenting everyday life. The abundance is overpowering and the archive seen is something that otherwise, would unlikely be shared.
