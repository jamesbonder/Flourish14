Olivia Richardson

BA (Hons) Photographic Art


About Olivia's Work

Olivia is an artist originally from Leeds, W.Yorkshire, now based in Cardiff. Her practise deals heavily in the issues of family, religion and personal experience often using found objects and archive images and showing them in a new and different light.

Hair has been called the external soul with the virtues and properties of a person said to be concentrated within their hair. Our lives naturally gather ‘stuff’. The unwanted can become a burden and throughout time the superfluous must be routinely removed to provide a clean slate – much like the hair on our heads.

Where does that unwanted matter go?

There is similarity in both the pursuit of perfection in our lives and in our hair. Just as we must (in many cases) lose some hair to achieve the look we desire; there are issues, problems and ‘things’ we must let go of if we are to live the life we want or deserve.

This work starts to unpick the seemingly everyday hairdressing experience; from the intimate and private information we willingly divulge to the physical pieces of ourselves we shed in name of self-improvement. With access to this environment through a lifelong family business, the work also looks at the personal struggles and burdens the hairdresser takes on in this everyday process. With readily discarded information shown threefold, this piece: compels an intimate analysis of that which has been previously disclosed and cast away; forces an interaction with the physical and beautifies the disposed of.
