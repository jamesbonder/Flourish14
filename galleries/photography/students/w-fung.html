Winnie Fung

BA (Hons) Photography for Fashion and Advertising


About Winnie's Work

An exploration of the human figure through ballet is conducted in Motion. This project is a collaboration between dancer and photographer. This combination of skills explores the relationship of form and pose of ballet through the removal of representation.

By removing the focus and delineation of the subject, the ballet form is engaged differently by diverging from the traditionally sharp detail present in dance photography. The creation of the silhouette is captured in the elongation and exaggeration of their bodies - the ballet dancer is both familiar and alien, and the injection of colour emphasises the denial of detail. A tension is created through the distorted amalgamation of dance and art photography.

The combination of form and colour is realised through the enlargement and scale of the prints. Motion is simultaneously still and animated through its sequencing. Motion does not describe a linear choreography - rather, the jarred sequencing captures an essence of the ballet form.
