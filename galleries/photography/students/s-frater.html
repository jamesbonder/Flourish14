About Sian's Work

My name is Sian Frater and I have just graduated in BA(Hons) Photography for Fashion and Advertising from University of Wales, Newport. For my Final Major Project with the University, I have studied how women adapt themselves for the working world in a project titled "Uniformed Women". 'Since World War II, women have been able to set their own desired career path. They have had to adapt themselves to be a part of the working world from being a full-time homemaker, to` begin to enjoy the independence which comes from producing their own income.

Then women began to fight for their own equal rights throughout the late 20th century in order to be paid the same as men as they were working just as hard. Now, in the 21st century, women can feel pressured to have a distinguished career as well as the perfect family, due to how women are represented in the media. As I am currently finding myself evolving to fit into the working woman lifestyle and adapting my personality to fit within a company, is this implicating a cost to my own personality or is this who I truly am? Is this a circumstance that all women will face once they leave their childhood behind?...'
