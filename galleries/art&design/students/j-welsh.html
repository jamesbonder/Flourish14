About Jessica's Work

My name is Jessica Welsh and I am currently in my third year studying Interior Design at the university of South Wales. I have always been passionate about interior design, and am known for often walking into buildings and checking out the structure of the interior and materials they have used, before noticing the contents of the place.

Many of my projects provide interiors and designs that offer an experience in themselves, and provide interactive elements depending on the brief. For example, the brief for the project shown here was to create a scheme which would improve well-being within the workplace. My design provided a garden and allotment space on the roof of a building, where workers could go on their lunch break to chill out and enjoy the outdoors, but also pick fruit and vegetables to take home for their supper that evening.

I also designed a structure and seating area where meetings could be taken outside in a much less formal setting. Much of my work I draw by hand as to me it feels much more organic, which in turn makes me hppier with the work I produce. I am currently still trying to perfect my style of drawing and presentation, but due to me being a perfectionist, I am always trying new styles and medium to get the best possible results.
