About Rosie's Work

My work is about story telling and Off The Grid is an exploration of ways in which fashion can be displayed through the use of a narrative.

One of the main problems for students, especially those studying creative subjects, is budget restraints especially when paying for lavish ways in which to introduce their work to the public. As part of my final major project, inspired by both installation art and land art I have created a series of installations in the aim to deliver a beautiful display of work with a carefully thought out narrative to support it.

Taking a conceptual approach to the display of fashion, the aim of the installation is to present garments in an alternative way to a conventional gallery experience. Decontexualising the work to re-appropriate the original meaning as part of a narrative, to be viewed and appreciated by the public, as well as making a statement, with virtually no costs.

Through collaboration with friend and designer, Kathryn Lewis, Off The Grid is a conceptual journey where by the themes addressed and the character portrayed within the concept act as a metaphor for the creative process behind the work and the role I have adopted in order to deliver the final outcome.

After completing my degree. I hope to see as much of the world as possible and eventually pursue a career in Curation by applying for the MA Fashion Curation course at London College of Fashion.
