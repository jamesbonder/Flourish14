Michelle Eyvanaki

BA (Hons) Fashion Design


About Michelle's Work

University of South Wales graduate Michelle Eyvanaki specialises in innovative Womenswear following the ideology of clean cut finishes and luxurious quality sourced fabrics. The garments are made to look and feel good on the wearer and also to be built upon as cushioning cocooning comfort. The clothing is known for its beautifully elegant designs with architectural twists in shape and within the colour palettes. With this bold narrative in mind, the clothing has been built for hard working city based women, who will buy classical pieces that can be kept and worn for many years, and keep updated with applying and taking away layers.

Michelle Eyvanaki's debut Autumn/Winter 2015 Collection touches upon two stories, ‘Architectural Reclamation’ and ‘Metamorphosis Notion’, exploring the concept of comfort and shape; particularly with the heavy outer layer garments as they envelope and cocoon the garments and wearer within.

The architectural theme explores the out of the norm; structures that have shape and movement to them. The collection’s matte colour palette has been selected from architecture based around the city of Manchester and the heavily hung fabrics reflect upon the strong structured silhouettes of the workings that have been explored. The second theme, metamorphosis, explores the concept of cocooning. The protection it provides and the cushioning effect the cocoon has on the butterfly as it evolves within. The two themes work hand in hand as the collection allows the wear to both build upon their shape yet also feel it.
