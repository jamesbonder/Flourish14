Brittany Sims

BA (Hons) Photography


About Brittany's Work

I have studied photography, from A level to diploma and now degree level, for around seven years and throughout my time of studying photography i have used it as a tool to look inwards and discover who i really am. This at times has been difficult but through the use of photography i have gained an understanding of my true self. For my major project my theme is my life and my early childhood which has been a blur; due to an accident at the age of five where I suffered a head injury the memories of my early life have become forgotten.

The memories I do have are from others and their recall of events or they have been created through the use of family photographs. ‘Blackened Memories’ uses found family photographs, text and contemporary images to explore the notion of forgotten memories.

Through this body of work I have tried to explore the blackness and confusion I feel when trying to look back on my early years. Through the use of photography I have been able to revisit locations and times that hold memories to me. The memories I do have may be constructions of the truth but they are my own interpretation of the times and memories I have lived without.
